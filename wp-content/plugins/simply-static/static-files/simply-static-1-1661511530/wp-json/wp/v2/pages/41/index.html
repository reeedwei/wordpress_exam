The history of technology<\/strong> is the development over time of systematic techniques for making and doing things. The term technology<\/em>, a combination of the Greek term techn<\/i>, \u201cart, craft,\u201d with logos<\/em>, \u201cword, speech,\u201d meant in Greece a discourse on the arts, both fine and applied. When it first appeared in English in the 17th century, it was used to mean a discussion of the applied arts only, and gradually these \u201carts\u201d themselves came to be the object of the designation<\/a>. By the early 20th century, the term embraced a growing range of means, processes, and ideas in addition to tools<\/a> and machines<\/a>. By mid-century, technology<\/a> was defined by such phrases as \u201cthe means or activity by which man seeks to change or manipulate his environment.\u201d Even such broad definitions have been criticized by observers who point out the increasing difficulty of distinguishing between scientific inquiry and technological activity.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
